Powerlifting Analysis on IPF Tournaments¶

Powerlifting is a strength-based sport where athletes compete by performing three compound movements: squat, bench press, and deadlift. Powerlifting tournaments are hosted at different levels (locally, state, regionally, nationally, and internationally) and split into different divisions based on the athletes age, sex, and weight.

In this project we look at data for powerlifting tournaments. We will look specifically at tournaments hosted by the International Powerlifting Federation (IPF) as they are one of the largest organizations resposible for hosting quality, high-standard competitions internationally. With this data, we will look at current demographics of the sport such as participation amongst age groups, regions and strength levels. We can find trends in the sport's popularity geographically and between age groups. We will also create a model to project the strength of an athlete based on individual attributes like their height, weight, age, and sex.

As with any data analysis process, we start by obtaining the data from an open data source. Once the data is obtained, we will filter to look only at data from the IPF or their affilates. This is to ensure data integrity and accuracy of the analysis. We will explore the current standings and spread of tournament data with respect to location, athlete information, or equipment. Afterwards we will attempt to predict athletes strength using regression algorithms.

Data Collection¶

We use Python3 and libraries such as pandas, numpy, matplotlib and scikit-learn to perform our analysis.

The dataset we will be using comes from the Openpowerlifting Data Service. They are a community of powerlifters who scrape the internet for tournament information and append it to their database. They provide a great service to the powerlifting community by creating a permanent archive of the world's powerlifting data and providing it in convenient formats.

Acquiring the Data¶

The data can be downloaded from the OpenPowerlifting site here. On the site, we can follow the sidebar to "Bulk CSV Downloads" and select which dataset we want to use. There is a complete dataset containing information from all tournaments and another which only contains information about tournaments hosted by the IPF or its affiliates. Additionally, there is documentation for the columns of the dataset if more information is desired. For our analysis, we will use the dataset with only IPF and affiliate information.

In this dataset, we will focus primarily on these columns:

  • Sex - Sex category athlete competed in
  • Event - Tells us which lifts the athlete competed in [S] - Squat, [B] - Bench, [D] - Deadlift (or some permutation of the three)
  • Age - Athlete's age in Kg
  • AgeClass - What age range they are categorized as under IPF rules
  • Division - Name of their division (based on age and body weight)
  • BodyweightKg - Athlete's weight in Kg
  • WeightClassKg - Athlete's weight class in Kg
  • Best3SquatKg, Best3BenchKg, Best3DeadliftKg - Their heaviest successful lift out of three attempts
  • TotalKg - The total of their heaviest three successful lifts (empty if one of three lifts failed). This will also be our main indicator of an athlete's strength.
  • Country - Home country of lifter
  • MeetCountry - Country the tournament is located in
  • Date

As per the IPF technical rule book, athletes are first divided by sex, then age group, and finally weight.

We now recreate these same classes using IPF standards. For males, their age class is split from

  • Open, All age groups 14 and above
  • Sub-Junior, day he reaches 14 to 18
  • Junior, day he reaches 19 to 23
  • Master I, day he reaches 40 to 49
  • Master II, day he reaches 50 to 59
  • Master III, day he reaches 60 to 69
  • Master IV, 70 and upwards

For females, similar divisions

  • Open, All age groups 14 and above
  • Sub-Junior, day he reaches 14 to 18

  • Master I, day he reaches 40 to 49
  • Master II, day he reaches 50 to 59
  • Master III, day he reaches 60 to 69
  • Master IV, 70 and upwards
